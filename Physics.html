Energy for Movement

Energy and It's Source

Humans and animals source their energy from food. The energy in the food originally came from the Sun but today we can directly use energy from the sun (solar energy) to heat water and to produce electricity. Anything that is moving contains a store of kinetic energy.

Energy Stored

One way to store large amounts of energy is by using fossil fuels but since they are non-renewable, they will run out one day. The energy that is released by burning fuels can be transferred by heating which is normally used for cooking or keeping the house warm.

Energy stored in raised objects is called gravitational potential energy. Internal/thermal energy is stored in the movement of particles, mostly in hot things. Heating is a way of transferring energy from hot objects to cooler ones.

Types of Energy

There are 10 major types of energy which include thermal energy, radiant energy, chemical energy, nuclear energy, electrical energy, motion energy, sound energy, elastic energy, and gravitational energy with the first four on the list being potential energy and the last six on the list being kinetic energy.

The Law of Conservation of Energy

The law of conservation of energy states that energy cannot be created nor destroyed, it can only be transferred. Sometimes when transferred, not all the energy will come with. When energy is being used, it doesn't just disappear, it will change from one form to another. For example, when a microwave oven is being used, the energy will change from electrical energy to thermal energy.

Efficiency

The greater the efficiency, the less energy is wasted. The efficiency of energy compares the useful energy transferred to the total energy transferred and wasted energy is usually transferred to the surroundings by heating and sound or, we can just simply say that the energy is dissipated.
