Indonesia's Very Own Sustainable City

Balikpapan

Balikpapan is a city on the east coast of Borneo Island in the Indonesian province of East Kalimantan. The city puts the environment and sustainability as their top priority as their vision is to become a livable and sustainable city.

Fun facts about Balikpapan!

- Balikpapan is home to one of the largest oil refineries in Indonesia.
- Balikpapan is a popular destination for eco-tourism.
- The city is home to various wildlife rehabilitation centers.

Their Sustainability Efforts

For this goal to be achieved, the Balikpapan government issued several environmental policies and regulations which include:
- issued pro-environment policies (such as prohibiting excessive coal mining)
- 52% of the city is dedicated to being a protected forest
- City officials and corporations have regular meetings to review and update the agenda
- Successfully done a Waste-to-Energy program where they turned methane gas into energy for household units

Achievements

Their pursuit of sustainability has earned several national and international recognitions such as the Most Loved City award by the WWF Earth Hour City Challenge 2015 and the highest award for the cleanest city in Indonesia by Adipura Kencana for five consecutive years.
